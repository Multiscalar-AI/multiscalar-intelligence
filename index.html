Synthetic Societies of Minds

Teaching Machines to Coordinate

Multiscalar AI develops new algorithms and systems for multi-agent intelligence, enabling agents to learn, coordinate, and scale from individuals to open networks. Our work focuses on emerging scaling paradigms for coordination: training agents to reason strategically, enabling collective learning across coalitions, and building the foundations for large-scale coordination in open, adversarial environments.

Research
01

Socio Cognitive Pressure

Studying how social dynamics like reputation, competition, and peer review create the pressure that squeezes more intelligence out of LLM agents, mirroring the mechanisms that scale human cognition.

02

Mechanism Design

Drawing from game theory and economics to engineer the rules, incentives, and constraints under which multi agent societies produce emergent intelligence that exceeds any individual member.

03

Evaluation Arenas

Building benchmarks and adversarial environments to rigorously measure when and why synthetic societies produce the "1+1=3" effect, and when they don't.

04

Orchestration & Monitoring

Developing the infrastructure and tooling to run, observe, and debug synthetic societies of agents at scale, from multi agent debate to full division of labour simulations.
